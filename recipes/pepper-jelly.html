Mom's Pepper Jelly

<-Recipes Home

About this Recipe

Another dead simple recipe with only five ingredients and a handful of steps. This hot pepper jelly is great for toast, charcuterie spreads and crudites.

Not only is this recipe MUCH cheaper than buying from the store, it also lasts longer. Although, I'm not sure how long it will last once you get a taste of it for the first time. I've been known to eat the entire jar in one sitting.

Ingredients

  • 1 ghost pepper, minced
  • 6 oz japapenos (keep seeds for extra hot)/li>
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 3 cups sugar
  • 3 oz liquid pectin

Method

  1. Put hald of the japapenos, 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar in a blender and blend well.
  2. In a pot, combine remaining jalapeno peppers, vinegar, blended mix, and sugar and beat over medium heat until just before it starts to boil.
  3. Add minched ghost pepper (this is what adds the pretty color) and stir constantly.
  4. Once at a rolling boil add the liquid pectin and boil for one more minute.
  5. Save a nice big glass jar to put it in. This backes about 2 1/2 cups.

Notes

Keep the seeds in the jalapenos to make itextra hot!
